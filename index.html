My Services

Project Management

Project Management professional with experience in the use of Lean, Six Sigma and Process improvement. Winner of Veterans Posse Scholarship, matriculating as pioneers of a dynamic new leadership program at Vassar College.Specialized experience in Business Development, Customer Service, Risk Management, Preventative Maintenance and Safety.

Lean 6 Sigma 

Experienced Black belt with multiple successful projects and Yellow Belt teaching experience. Founded several teams and completed projects for enterprise level organizations in record time, while uplifting employees and empowering them to produce change. 

Team Building

Trained in goal setting and team building exercises by leading organizations such as the US Army, The Posse Foundation, and the Fullbridge Program, Moody’s Corporation, Texas Leadership Forum, to name a few. Founded and led teams to produce ground breaking results in various industries, such as manufacturing, nonprofit work, and military and medical administration. 

I am Anu Datta

Proud Veteran. Project Manager. Philanthropist.

It all began as a glint in my fathers eye…just kidding. The pursuit of the American Dream brought me from the sweltering heat of Calcutta, India to the winding streets of San Francisco, CA, and I haven’t stopped chasing it since. At Oak Ridge Military Academy, I was the fastest promoted cadet, going from private to 1st Lieutenant within less than a year. In the United States Army, I prepared and led my team on countless missions to support OEF/OIF. As a civilian, I passionately pursued education and training programs to learn and grow at every opportunity.

They say a person should always have 2 things : a mentor and a protege, and I was lucky enough to find both.  It is my personal mission to shatter glass ceilings, and leave the door open behind me. I hope to secure employment with a company that is aligned with my values, goals, and vision for a better future.

"A career in modeling provides plenty of tough-but-valuable lessons for the would-be entrepreneur. It requires discipline, the ability to handle rejection and brush of skepticism and critics at every pass; resilience is key. Models must exude self-confidence, while remaining constantly aware of their own flaws and shortcomings. Critically, the model is, from day one, her own rainmaker. She might have to go on hundreds of “go sees” before booking a single job or collecting a single paycheck, so the job is a daily lesson in persistence. Almost by definition, a successful model must build an identifiable “brand” long before she lends her name to anything."

-Kathy Ireland for Forbes Magazine
